## Q60 ANSWERED: Why Dimensions 1, 2, 4, 8?

### Hurwitz's Theorem (1898)

The **only** normed division algebras over the reals are:

| Algebra | Dimension | Properties |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| R (reals) | 1 | Commutative, associative, ordered |
| C (complex) | 2 | Commutative, associative |
| H (quaternions) | 4 | **Non-commutative**, associative |
| O (octonions) | 8 | **Non-commutative, non-associative** |

### Why These Are The Only Options

**Multiple independent proofs:**

1. **Clifford Algebra Approach** (Lee 1948, Chevalley 1954)
   - Operators form Clifford algebra
   - Representation dimensions constrain N
   - Only 1, 2, 4, 8 satisfy the constraints

2. **Cayley-Dickson Construction**
   - Start with R, double to get C, double to get H, double to get O
   - Each doubling **loses a property**:
     - C loses ordering
     - H loses commutativity
     - O loses associativity
   - Further doubling loses the **norm property**
   - **No more division algebras possible!**

3. **Topological** (Adams 1958, Bott-Milnor 1958)
   - Related to vector fields on spheres
   - Homotopy groups of classical Lie groups
   - Only 1, 2, 4, 8 work

**These dimensions are MATHEMATICAL NECESSITY, not physical choice.**

**Confidence: VERY HIGH (proven theorem)**
